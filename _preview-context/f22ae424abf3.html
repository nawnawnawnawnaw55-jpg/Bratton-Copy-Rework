What Is Shoulder Impingement?

Shoulder impingement occurs when the tendons of the rotator cuff become compressed (or "pinched") between the bones of the shoulder during movement. This can lead to irritation, inflammation, and pain.

What Causes Shoulder Impingement?

This condition often develops gradually and is commonly related to movement patterns and overuse.

Common causes include:

  • ✓ Repetitive overhead activities (sports, work, lifting)
  • ✓ Poor posture (rounded shoulders)
  • ✓ Muscle imbalances or weakness
  • ✓ Tight shoulder structures
  • ✓ Previous shoulder injuries

Signs and Symptoms

  • ▸ Pain when lifting the arm overhead
  • ▸ Discomfort when reaching behind your back
  • ▸ Shoulder pain that worsens with activity
  • ▸ Weakness or limited range of motion
  • ▸ Pain when lying on the affected side

How Physical Therapy Helps

Pain Relief

  • ✓ Activity modification to reduce irritation
  • ✓ Ice, heat, or other therapeutic techniques

Restoring Mobility

  • ✓ Stretching tight muscles and tissues
  • ✓ Improving shoulder and upper back flexibility

Strengthening Muscles

  • ✓ Exercises for the rotator cuff and shoulder blade muscles
  • ✓ Correcting muscle imbalances

Improving Movement & Posture

  • ✓ Training proper shoulder mechanics
  • ✓ Postural correction to reduce stress on the joint

Benefits of Physical Therapy

  • ✓ Reduces pain and inflammation
  • ✓ Restores normal movement
  • ✓ Improves strength and posture
  • ✓ Helps prevent recurrence

When to Seek Help

If shoulder pain is limiting your daily activities or not improving with rest, physical therapy can help you recover and move comfortably again.
